Talk track — current state
Opening (lead with the frame)
"This is how May leads actually moved. Two lanes, left to right. Channels on the left feed both funnels. The Standard lane — the top row — is working the best it ever has. The Lite self-serve lane — the bottom row — has a structural break right in the middle."
Standard lane (~45 seconds)
"235 form submits — intentionally lower year-over-year. We rerouted T3/T4 leads away from the demo form last quarter, so the volume drop is by design. What matters is quality: T1/T2 share is now 40%, up from 20% a year ago. 100 demos completed with a 47% show rate — nearly doubled year-over-year. The demo engine is the strongest it's ever been. The SMS reminder sequence Sales shipped in April lifted show rate by 10 points, and better-fit leads showing up from our improved quality filters is the upstream input. 47 new Standard trialists, 33 to 34 gross new Standard logos — flat year-over-year, acceptable given the quality improvement."
Lite lane (~60 seconds)
"8,362 visits to the Lite landing page and Pricing page combined. Real traffic, real intent. The problem is what happens next. [Point to the red BREAK node] 616 people submitted the screening form — and then disappeared. Only 32 reached registration. 1 opened a verification email. Zero reached payment. The screening-form gate is the break. Users complete one form, then are asked to complete another before getting into the product. They abandon. Fullstory confirmed 45 people stuck at the password step in real time. The ~50 Lite trialists you see downstream — those are coming through the Pricing page, which has a working flow. 70 completions in May at 2.15% conversion. That's the proof demand exists. The LP path is just broken."
If challenged on conversion numbers vs Mallory:
Marketing reports gross new logos — first invoice paid. That's what we control. Mallory's deck uses 'held to 2nd invoice' — a CX retention signal. Both are real. Different gates, different questions. We're working on aligning to one shared definition. Until then, I'm reporting what I can defend.
If challenged on channel attribution:
Channel splits are from HubSpot logo attribution. About 24% of Standard and 30% of Lite have no channel assigned — that's our UTM debt. Fixing it is on the Q3 roadmap. The directional story holds: Brand Search is the top channel for both lanes, Facebook is second, and we have real paid campaign data from Meta that confirms both lanes are being served.

Talk track — future state
Opening (connect to previous slide)
"The previous slide showed the break. This slide shows the fix — and what the numbers look like when it ships. Nothing changes in the Standard lane. That's the point — the Standard funnel is working and we're not touching it. What changes is the entire Lite self-serve path."
The fix (~60 seconds on the two new nodes)
"Instead of routing visitors to a 'start trial now' CTA that breaks on a screening form, we replace it with gated content — a quiz, a business plan template, or a setup checklist. The visitor gets something valuable. We capture their email. Then we put them into a 3-email nurture sequence. When they show trial-ready signals — opens, clicks, engagement — we route them to trial. This isn't hypothetical. We have almost all the assets already: Annabelle's quiz with a 3-email nurture already built, five resource guides from 2024, Rylan's partner checklist. Net-new content only where needed. Sprint 3 starts today — we're planning the content offers and picking vertical 1. Sprint 4 builds and wires it. July 7 it goes live."
The numbers (uplift bar at top)
"Same 8,362 visits. No new spend required. We go from 0 paid completions to a target ~2% conversion — that's the Pricing page's validated rate, which we know works because 70 people completed it in May. Apply that to all 8,362 visits and you get ~167 Lite trials per month. At current trial-to-won rates, that's 40 to 70 gross new Lite logos per month versus 22 today. The ~167 is directional. Actual rate depends on content offer quality and nurture effectiveness. But the floor is real because the Pricing page already proves the demand."
If asked: "Is the 2% conversion rate realistic?"
It's not a projection — it's an observed rate from the Pricing page, which had 70 completions out of 3,258 visits in May. We're applying a known working rate to a larger traffic base. If the gated content converts better, we beat 167. If worse, we land lower. Either way, we go from zero to something.
If asked: "How confident are you in the July 7 date?"
Confident in the sprint structure — Sprint 3 and 4 are planned and staffed. July 7 is the Lite LP going live with gated content and nurture triggered. We'll have early data on start-of-flow lift before the August report.
If Alison raises CX coverage capacity:
More Lite trialists flowing in is a good problem to have — but it does mean CX will need to prioritise which accounts to touch. Alison's data shows CX reaches about 10% of their active book per month. The fix opens the tap; CX coverage is the downstream constraint. That's a Q3 planning conversation.
