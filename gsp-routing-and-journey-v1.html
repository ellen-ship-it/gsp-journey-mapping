From form submit to customer

Two linked views of the same person. First, the front-of-funnel routing that decides what they see and who owns them the moment they submit a form. Then, the lifecycle journey that tracks them to a plan and beyond. The two connect at one point — the demo-form submission.

Read this first — three things to reconcile

These are the gaps between the journey map and the live routing logic. They're framed for your review; you can hide this band for the meeting itself.

SPLIT POINTS

There are two, not one

The experience forks at form submit — which scheduler, which owner. The plan of record forks later, at trial start. Earlier drafts read as one shared funnel until trial; that's true for the plan, but the experience has already diverged by the MQL stage.

Fix: show the fork twice — soft at the form, locked at trial.
TERMINOLOGY

Routed-Lite ≠ Lite plan

Same word, two layers. A lead sent to the Lite scheduler can still start a Standard trial, and vice versa. Routing is the experience at form submit; plan is recorded at trial. In reporting, don't tie route counts to plan-of-record counts — they won't reconcile.

Fix: never report a "Lite" number without saying which layer.
MISSING LANES

Spanish & INTL exist

The clean two-lane (Lite / Standard) model leaves out two real destinations. Spanish and international are locale pre-checks that run before the matrix and skip it entirely — five destinations total, not two.

Fix: add both as pre-matrix routes in the funnel.

The one thing not to conflate

Routed-Lite is not the same as a Lite-plan customer

In view 01, "Lite" means the experience and owner assigned at form submit. In view 02, "Lite" means the plan of record set at trial start (core_account_plan_at_last_trial_start). A person can be routed to the Lite scheduler and still start a Standard trial. Because the plan is recorded as early as trial — not at conversion — trial-stage reporting must already split Lite vs Standard. If a report splits only at conversion, the trial and conversion numbers will never tie out.
